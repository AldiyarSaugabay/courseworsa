Contenders Circle Star Forward Ahead of Trade Deadline

By Alex Turner • November 10, 2025 • 4 hours ago

Multiple playoff contenders have expressed serious interest in acquiring a veteran star forward as the NBA trade deadline approaches, league sources tell SportsDirect. With several teams jockeying for position in a tightly packed playoff race, the forward’s two-way ability and expiring contract have made him one of the most sought-after names on the market.

Growing Market Interest

According to sources, the Milwaukee Bucks, Los Angeles Lakers, and Dallas Mavericks are among the franchises monitoring the situation closely. Each organization views the veteran as a potential final piece to solidify its postseason rotation and bolster frontcourt depth heading into the spring.

“He’s the kind of player who fits anywhere high IQ, defends multiple positions, and doesn’t need the ball to make an impact.”
Western Conference Executive

Contract Situation

The forward is in the final year of his deal, earning roughly 18 million this season. His team, currently on the outside looking in at the playoff picture, is said to be open to offers that include young assets or future draft compensation. With the front office facing key offseason decisions, moving him now could help reset their long-term strategy.

Points per Game 17.8

Possible Trade Destinations

The Lakers are believed to have had preliminary discussions centered around matching salaries, while the Mavericks could explore packaging rotational players and second-round picks. The Bucks, meanwhile, have limited flexibility but may engage in multi-team talks to make a move possible.

“If a contender lands him, it changes their ceiling,”
Eastern Conference Scout

Timing and Outlook

The trade deadline is less than three weeks away, and executives expect negotiations to intensify as teams gain clarity on their postseason trajectories. The player’s current franchise has expressed respect for his professionalism but is aware that his value may never be higher.

The Bottom Line

As the market heats up, all eyes will be on whether a deal materializes before the deadline buzzer sounds. For now, the veteran forward remains focused on playing his role while the rest of the league debates which contender will strike first.

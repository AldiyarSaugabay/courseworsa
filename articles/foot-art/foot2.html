Breaking: PSG Make Record Bid for Premier League Star

By David Chen • November 10, 2025 • 2 hours ago

Paris Saint-Germain have officially submitted a club-record transfer bid for one of the Premier League’s most sought-after players, signaling their intent to rebuild and reinforce ahead of the 2025–26 campaign. Sources close to the negotiations describe the offer as “historic,” with reports suggesting the deal could exceed €180 million including bonuses.

The Target Revealed

While PSG have not publicly named the player, multiple outlets confirm that Manchester City forward Marcus Evans is the subject of the bid. The 25-year-old England international has been a standout performer this season, scoring 24 goals and providing 14 assists across all competitions.

Evans, whose contract runs until 2028, is reportedly open to exploring new challenges abroad, though City remain reluctant to sell. The French champions see him as the centerpiece of their long-term project under sporting director Luís Campos.

“We are ambitious and want players who can define a generation Marcus Evans is one of them.”
PSG Sporting Director Luís Campos

Negotiations Underway

PSG’s opening bid is believed to be worth €150 million upfront, with an additional €30 million in performance-related add-ons. Manchester City are expected to review the offer over the coming days but have made it clear they will only consider a sale if the player himself pushes for the move.

According to insiders, Evans has not yet formally requested a transfer, but discussions with his representatives suggest he is intrigued by the prospect of becoming the new face of PSG’s post-Mbappé era.

Financial Fair Play Implications

PSG’s bid comes amid renewed scrutiny over UEFA Financial Fair Play (FFP) regulations. The club has worked aggressively to balance its books after high-profile exits last summer, including those of Kylian Mbappé and Neymar. Analysts believe the sale of several squad players and sponsorship restructuring have made this mega-offer feasible.

Transfer Breakdown

Transfer Fee €150M + €30M bonuses
Contract Length 5 years (until 2030)
Weekly Wage €600,000 (reported)

What This Means for PSG

After losing both Mbappé and Neymar, PSG have been desperate for a global star capable of restoring their attacking spark. Evans, known for his versatility, pressing, and creativity, fits perfectly into Luis Enrique’s system. His arrival would mark the beginning of a new era at the Parc des Princes.

“Marcus has all the qualities intelligence, power, and leadership. He would elevate any team in the world.”
Former PSG Captain Marquinhos

City’s Position

Manchester City are under no financial pressure to sell and value Evans as a key figure in their long-term plans. Pep Guardiola has privately expressed admiration for the player’s development and is unlikely to sanction his departure unless the player explicitly asks to leave.

With the Premier League champions eyeing another treble, losing one of their main attacking threats would be a major blow both on and off the pitch.

What Happens Next

Negotiations are expected to continue this week, with PSG confident of reaching an agreement before the transfer window officially opens. Evans is currently on international duty with England, and his representatives have yet to comment publicly.

Whether this record-breaking offer turns into a completed transfer remains to be seen, but one thing is certain PSG have sent shockwaves through European football once again.
